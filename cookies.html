Cookie Policy

Last Updated: 31-08-2025

What Are Cookies?

As is common practice with almost all professional websites, this site uses cookies, which are tiny files that are downloaded to your computer to improve your experience. This page describes what information they gather, how we use it, and why we sometimes need to store these cookies. We will also share how you can prevent these cookies from being stored, however, this may downgrade or 'break' certain elements of the site's functionality.

Cookies can be "Persistent" or "Session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your personal computer or mobile device when you go offline, while Session cookies are deleted as soon as you close your web browser.

How We Use Cookies

This website, and the third-party services it uses, place cookies on your browser for several reasons, including:

  • To serve personalized advertisements.
  • To enable the functionality of embedded content, such as videos.
  • To ensure the security and performance of the website.

Because we do not have user accounts or a shopping cart, we do not use cookies for remembering login details or managing order processing.

Third-Party Cookies

In some special cases, we also use cookies provided by trusted third parties. The following section details which third-party cookies you might encounter through this site.

  • Google AdSense: We use Google AdSense to serve advertising. The Google AdSense service uses a DoubleClick cookie to serve more relevant ads across the web and limit the number of times that a given ad is shown to you. For more information on how Google handles your data, please see the Google Privacy Policy. You can opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google's Ad Settings.
  • YouTube: We embed videos from YouTube on our site. When you view a page containing an embedded YouTube video, YouTube may set a number of cookies on your browser. These cookies are used for functionality (e.g., remembering your volume settings) and for collecting analytics and showing personalized advertising within the video player.
  • Cloudflare: We use Cloudflare for website hosting, security, and performance. Cloudflare may use "strictly necessary" cookies to support its security features, such as identifying individual clients behind a shared IP address and applying security settings on a per-client basis. These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information.

Your Choices Regarding Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie rights in the following ways:

  • Cookie Consent Banner: When you first visit our site, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or reject the use of non-essential cookies.
  • Browser Controls: You can set or amend your web browser controls to accept or refuse cookies. If you choose to reject cookies, you may still use our website, though your access to some functionality and areas may be restricted. As the means by which you can refuse cookies through your web browser controls vary from browser to browser, you should visit your browser's help menu for more information.
